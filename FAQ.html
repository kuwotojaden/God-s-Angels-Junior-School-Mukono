Common Questions

Find answers to frequently asked questions about our school

What grades does God's Angels Junior School serve?

God's Angels Junior School serves students from Baby Class (ages 3-4) through Primary 4 (ages 9-10). We provide a nurturing environment for children in their early developmental years.

What is the school's approach to Christian education?

Our Christ-centered approach integrates biblical principles into all aspects of learning. We believe that faith and learning are interconnected, and we strive to help students develop both academically and spiritually. Daily prayer, Bible study, and character development are integral parts of our curriculum.

What are the school hours and calendar?

Regular school hours are Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM. We follow the national academic calendar with breaks during Easter, Christmas, and summer. Specific holiday dates are published in the school calendar distributed at the beginning of each academic year.

How can I apply for admission?

To apply for admission, visit our Admissions page to download the application form. You will need to provide your child's birth certificate, previous school records (if applicable), immunization records, and two passport photos. After submitting your application, you will be contacted for an interview and assessment.

What safety measures are in place?

Safety is our top priority. Our campus is secured with controlled access gates, security cameras, and 24/7 monitoring. All visitors must check in at the main office. Our staff undergoes background checks and regular safety training. We maintain a 1:10 teacher-to-student ratio in all classes.

Are there extracurricular activities available?

Yes, we offer various extracurricular activities including music, art, drama, sports, and technology clubs. These programs are designed to develop students' talents and interests beyond the classroom. Participation is encouraged and often integrated into our regular schedule.

What are the school's discipline policies?

We use a positive discipline approach focusing on character development and conflict resolution. Our policies emphasize respect, responsibility, and kindness. When issues arise, we work with students to understand consequences and learn from their actions. We involve parents as partners in addressing behavioral concerns.

Is there transportation available?

Yes, we provide school bus services to surrounding neighborhoods. Routes and schedules are published before the start of each academic year. Transportation fees are separate from tuition and are charged per semester. Contact our administration office for route information and availability.
